A. Summary of Key Facts
1. Westminster's officer, Malak Al-Mosawi, confirmed in writing: "Priority Need: YES" [BM-3]. Section 188(1) HA 1996 uses the word "shall" — not "may." Once Priority Need is confirmed, the duty to accommodate is mandatory and immediate. Westminster provided nothing.
2. On 29 August 2025, the Claimant disclosed to Westminster officer Judy that he could be dead by Monday. The officer's verbatim response, as recorded at [BM-1]: "If you're not dead, would you like the appointment on Monday or not." No crisis referral was made. No safeguarding alert was raised. No accommodation was arranged.
3. Following the recording at [BM-1], Malak Al-Mosawi denied in a subsequent communication [BM-2] that the statement was made. The recording proves it was. This is not officer error — a genuine error does not involve a specific denial of a recorded event. In Watkins v Secretary of State for the Home Department [2006] 2 AC 395, misfeasance in public office requires knowing unlawfulness.
4. 319 verified Monzo bank transactions [EXH-SD-001] document every hostel, emergency accommodation, and survival payment made over 505 days. The sum of £6,613.81 represents transaction-level evidence of Westminster's undischarged mandatory duty. CPR 25.7(1)(c) permits an interim payment where the claimant would obtain judgment for a substantial amount at trial. Westminster have admitted Priority Need in writing.
5. The Claimant is autistic (diagnosed Dr Catherine Chung, CLAAS NHS Trust, 11 April 2023 [BM-MED-001]). Section 20 EA 2010 required Westminster to make reasonable adjustments to their homelessness assessment process. No adjustments were made despite written notification of diagnosis.
6. Between September and November 2024: Liverpool City Council took Abel (18/27 September 2024, Exhibits 18 & 27); Westminster City Council processed eviction while the High Court application was active (7 November 2024, Exhibits 20 & 21). The Claimant was conducting advocacy in three courts simultaneously while street homeless. This is documented at skeleton argument paragraph 115.
B. Relief Sought
7. The Claimant respectfully asks this Court to:
(a)confirm the without-notice injunction filed 26 March 2026;
(b)order Westminster City Council to pay £6,613.81 within 7 days (CPR 25.7(1)(c));
(c)alternatively, order Westminster to provide interim accommodation forthwith under s.188(1) HA 1996;
(d)order that Westminster be forbidden from withdrawing any accommodation pending final determination; and
(e)costs in the cause.

I.I am the Claimant in these proceedings. I make this statement in reply to any opposition Westminster City Council may raise to my without-notice application for an injunction (N16A) and interim payment (CPR 25.7(1)(c)), filed 26 March 2026.
II.I am autistic (diagnosed Dr Catherine Chung, CLAAS NHS Trust, 11 April 2023 [BM-MED-001]). I am filing as a litigant in person from the street, having been without stable housing for 505 days as a direct result of Westminster's admitted failure to discharge their s.188(1) Housing Act 1996 duty.
A. THE DUTY ADMISSION CANNOT BE RETRACTED
III.Westminster's officer Malak Al-Mosawi confirmed in writing: "Priority Need: YES" [BM-3]. This is not a disputed fact — it is Westminster's own written record. Section 188(1) HA 1996 uses the word "shall" — not "may." Once Priority Need is confirmed, the duty to accommodate is mandatory and immediate. Westminster did not comply with it.
IV.If Westminster argues that the duty was discharged by any subsequent communication, I invite them to produce written evidence of accommodation actually offered and refused. They cannot. The only "offer" was a future appointment — made after a death disclosure.
B. THE DEATH DISCLOSURE AND RESPONSE
V.On 29 August 2025, I disclosed to Westminster officer Judy that I could be dead by Monday. Her verbatim response, as recorded at [BM-1]: "If you're not dead, would you like the appointment on Monday or not." No crisis referral was made. No safeguarding alert was raised. No accommodation was arranged.
C. MISFEASANCE — DENIAL OF RECORDED FACT
VI.Following the recording at [BM-1], Malak Al-Mosawi denied in a subsequent communication [BM-2] that the statement was made. The original recording proves she did. This is not officer error. In Watkins v Secretary of State for the Home Department [2006] 2 AC 395, misfeasance requires knowing unlawfulness. The denial of a documented, recorded statement satisfies that test.
D. QUANTUM — 319 TRANSACTIONS
VII.319 verified Monzo bank transactions [EXH-SD-001] document every hostel, emergency accommodation, and survival payment made over 505 days. This is not an estimate — it is transaction-level evidence. Westminster's mandatory duty existed throughout. The sum of £6,613.81 represents the documented cost of their failure.
VIII.CPR 25.7(1)(c) permits an interim payment where the court is satisfied that the claimant would obtain judgment for a substantial amount at trial. Westminster have admitted Priority Need in writing. There is no viable defence to liability. The interim payment of £6,613.81 is 0.9% of the Scott Schedule quantum of £729,839 [BM-6].
E. DISABILITY — REASONABLE ADJUSTMENTS REFUSED
IX.I am autistic. Section 20 EA 2010 required Westminster to make reasonable adjustments to their homelessness assessment process. No adjustments were made despite written notification of my diagnosis [BM-MED-001]. HMCTS themselves refused adjustments on 6 November 2025 [BM-RA-CALL] and 16 January 2026 [Exhibit 34].
F. SIMULTANEOUS INSTITUTIONAL OPERATION
X.Between September and November 2024: Liverpool City Council took Abel (Exhibits 18 & 27, 18/27 September 2024); Westminster City Council processed my eviction while my High Court application was active (Exhibits 20 & 21, 7 November 2024). I was fighting three courts simultaneously while street homeless. Documented at skeleton argument paragraph 115.
XI.This is not coincidence. The pattern of detriment clustering — accommodation withdrawal, care package cancellation, and child contact restriction — all occurring following formal legal advocacy, is documented across 57 verified exhibits spanning 2024–2026.
G. BALANCE OF CONVENIENCE
XII.The Claimant is street homeless, autistic, and without income. Westminster City Council has an annual housing budget in excess of £130 million. An order to pay £6,613.81 represents no material burden to Westminster and may be a matter of life or death to me. No cross-undertaking in damages is required from a publicly-funded litigant in person: Allen v Gulf Oil Refining Ltd [1981] AC 1001.
H. RELIEF SOUGHT
XIII.I respectfully ask this Court to:
(a)confirm the without-notice injunction dated 26 March 2026;
(b)order Westminster City Council to pay £6,613.81 within 7 days (CPR 25.7(1)(c));
(c)alternatively, order Westminster to provide interim accommodation forthwith under s.188(1) HA 1996;
(d)order Westminster be forbidden from withdrawing accommodation pending final determination; and
(e)costs in the cause.
